Security and Risk Management

This module will explore the various areas where security and risk overlap and interact with each other. Activities, lectures and projects had reviewed various methods of risk assessment and a deep dive into both qualitative and quantitative methods.

We discussed and explained a number of traditional risk models, such as STRIDE, DREAD and Attack Trees while also reviewing the increasing trend towards hybrid models. We also produced risk assessment reports for digitilisation containing common security standards such as PCI-DSS and look at business continuity and disaster recovery solutions. Finally, we discussed key research topics and advances driving the fields of security and risk management.

Learning Outcomes

This module involved activities, lectures and projects to:

  • Identify and analyse critically security risks, threats and vulnerabilities in information systems, accounting for the current threat landscape.
  • Gather and synthesise information from multiple sources (including internet security alerts & warning sites) to aid in the systematic analysis of risks & security issues manage and audit risk and security issues.
  • Critically determine appropriate methodologies, tools and techniques to mitigate and/or solve security risks and their business impact.
  • Articulate the legal, social, ethical, and professional issues faced by information security and risk professionals.
